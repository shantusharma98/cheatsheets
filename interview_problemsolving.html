The 6-Step Framework

Every analytics / strategy problem in an interview follows this universal structure. Master this, and you can answer any case question — from Netflix to Amazon.

Clarify Structure Hypothesize Analyze Root Cause Recommend
1
Step 01

Clarify the Problem

Before jumping in, ask smart clarifying questions to define the scope. This shows you think before you act — a key analyst trait.

What to ask

"What time period are we looking at? Is this a recent spike or a long-term trend? Is cancellation up across all segments or specific ones (age, region, plan)? What does success look like for our solution?"

2
Step 02

Structure Your Thinking (MECE)

Break the problem into Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive buckets. Use a tree or funnel to organize ideas visually. No overlapping, no gaps.

Example structure

Cancellations = f(Product issues | Price/Value perception | Competition | Life events | Poor targeting). Each bucket is separate and together they cover all causes.

3
Step 03

Form Hypotheses

Before looking at data, state what you think is happening and why. This shows hypothesis-driven thinking — the gold standard in consulting and analytics.

Example hypothesis

"I hypothesize the primary driver is price sensitivity following the recent price hike, compounded by a weak content slate in Q3 — particularly in the 18–34 age segment."

4
Step 04

Identify Key Metrics & Data

Define what data you'd pull to test your hypothesis. Be specific — table names, fields, segmentation. Show SQL/analytics intuition.

Key metrics to track

Churn Rate (monthly), Retention Rate by cohort, Time-to-cancel after signup, NPS score, Feature usage before cancel, Price plan at cancellation, Exit survey data, Engagement score (hours watched/week).

5
Step 05

Diagnose Root Causes

Use your data to confirm or reject hypotheses. Segment the problem — which users, which regions, which cohort? Apply the 5 Whys technique to go deeper.

5 Whys example

Cancellations up → Why? Users in 25–35 bracket leaving → Why? They're churning within 30 days → Why? Low watch-time in first week → Why? No personalized onboarding → Why? Recommendation engine not warm yet.

6
Step 06

Recommend & Prioritize

Suggest 3–4 actionable solutions, ranked by impact vs effort. Always state trade-offs and mention how you'd measure success (A/B test, KPI targets).

Output format

"My top recommendation is improving the first-7-days experience via a personalized 'Start Here' row, measured by D7 retention rate. Quick win: pause-subscription option to reduce hard cancels. Long term: invest in content genres where churn is highest."

Netflix: Customer Retention Case

"You are a Strategy Analyst at Netflix. Cancellations have increased 15% YoY. Find out why and what to do."

1
Clarify

Ask Before You Dive In

Before analyzing, tell the interviewer: "I'd like to ask a few clarifying questions." This is expected and respected.

Your Questions

• Is this 15% increase global or specific to a region (US, India, EU)?
• Is it across all plans (Basic, Standard, Premium) or one tier?
• Did anything change recently — price hike, content release schedule, a competitor launch?
• Do we have exit survey data or just churn events?
• Are we focusing on voluntary or involuntary churn (failed payments)?

2
Structure

Build a MECE Cancellation Tree

Organize all possible reasons into clean, non-overlapping buckets:

Cancellation Cause Tree

A. Product/Content Issues → Not enough new titles, poor recommendations, bad UI/UX, streaming quality

B. Price/Value Mismatch → Price increase, cheaper competitor, feels too expensive for usage level

C. External/Competition → Disney+, Prime, Max gaining users, free alternatives

D. Lifecycle / Seasonal → Binge-and-quit pattern, finished a show, seasonal (summer cord-cutting)

E. Technical / Operational → Payment issues, account sharing crackdown backlash

3
Hypothesize

State Your Best Guess Early

Don't wait for data to form an opinion. Show analytical confidence.

Strong hypothesis

"Given the 15% YoY jump aligns with Netflix's Q1 2023 password-sharing crackdown and price increases, I hypothesize the primary driver is perceived value erosion among casual, shared-account users — likely in the 18–35 segment who had lower individual willingness to pay."

4
Metrics & Data

Define What You'd Measure

Core KPIs to pull

• Churn Rate = Cancellations this month / Total subscribers start of month
• Retention Curve = % of month-0 subscribers still active at M1, M3, M6, M12
• Time-to-Cancel = Days between signup and cancel (new vs old users)
• Pre-cancel Engagement = Avg hours watched in last 14 days before cancel
• Exit Reason = Top categories from exit survey
• Reactivation Rate = % of churned users who returned within 90 days

5
Root Cause

Diagnose With Segmentation

Segment the data to find where the problem is concentrated. This is where you show analytical depth.

Segmentation cuts

By Plan: Is churn higher on Basic (ad-supported)? → Price sensitivity signal
By Tenure: New subscribers cancelling within 30 days? → Onboarding/value problem
By Region: India/LatAm spiking? → Competition from local streamers
By Engagement: Low-watch-time users churning? → Content-fit problem
By Content Genre: Churn spikes after specific shows end? → Binge-and-quit

6
Recommend

Prioritized Action Plan

Quick Wins (0–3 months)

① Add a Pause Subscription option (reduces hard cancels by ~20% per industry benchmarks)
② Trigger personalized win-back emails at 7-day inactivity with curated content picks
③ Surface a "Your Top Picks This Week" notification on Day 3 of new signups

Medium Term (3–6 months)

④ Redesign onboarding flow — force genre selection, show a "Start Here" shelf
⑤ Launch lower-tier plan in price-sensitive markets (India, LatAm) at ₹99–149/mo
⑥ Add social/profile features for households to increase stickiness

Measure Success With

Run A/B test on pause feature → measure D30 retention lift. Target: reduce monthly churn from 2.8% → 2.2%. Track NPS score quarterly.

Metric Design

How would you define and measure "engagement" for a streaming platform?

MEDIUM ▾
Clarify First

Ask: "Engagement for what purpose — to predict churn, content success, or ad revenue?" The answer shapes your metric.

Core Metrics
  • Watch Hours / Week — primary depth signal
  • Completion Rate — % of shows/movies watched to completion
  • D7 / D30 Retention — are users returning after signup?
  • Session Frequency — how many times/week they open the app
  • Content Diversity Index — are they exploring new genres?
Composite Score

Combine into an Engagement Score = (Weekly Hours × 0.4) + (Completion Rate × 0.3) + (Session Frequency × 0.3). Flag users below threshold for re-engagement campaigns.

Root Cause Analysis

DAUs (Daily Active Users) on our app dropped 20% overnight. What happened?

HARD ▾
Step 1 — Rule Out Data Issues

First check if it's real: Was it a tracking/logging bug? Did the dashboard pipeline fail? Check raw event tables, not just dashboards.

Step 2 — Segment to Isolate
  • By Platform: iOS, Android, Web — is it one platform?
  • By Geography: One country spiked? → Outage, competitor launch, news event
  • By User Type: New vs returning users — is it acquisition or retention?
  • By Feature: Did a specific page/feature stop working?
Step 3 — Check External Events

Did a competitor launch yesterday? Was there a news scandal? Did an A/B test ship that broke the onboarding? Did a push notification stop sending?

Communicate

Tell the interviewer: "I'd escalate to engineering immediately while segmenting, not wait for the full analysis before acting."

Product Strategy

Should Netflix launch a free, ad-supported tier in India?

HARD ▾
Framework

Use a Pros / Cons / Risks structure across 3 dimensions: Revenue, Brand, Market Expansion.

Arguments FOR
  • India has 600M+ smartphone users — massive TAM at low willingness-to-pay
  • Ad revenue can offset lower ARPU — YouTube model
  • Free tier reduces friction for user acquisition → funnel to paid conversion
  • Hotstar, Zee5 are already free/cheap — Netflix is losing market share
Arguments AGAINST
  • Brand dilution — Netflix = premium, free may cheapen perception
  • Cannibalization of existing paid subscribers who downgrade
  • Ad sales infrastructure doesn't exist in India yet
Recommendation

Yes — but with guardrails: limit free tier to mobile-only, older content library, max 2 hours/day. A/B test in Tier 2/3 cities first where paid penetration is near zero.

A/B Testing

How would you design an A/B test to evaluate a new recommendation algorithm?

MEDIUM ▾
Define Success Metric First

Primary: Click-Through Rate (CTR) on recommended titles. Secondary: Watch Completion Rate, D30 Retention. Guard rail metric: Do NOT let total watch time drop.

Design
  • Randomly split users 50/50 (control = old algorithm, treatment = new)
  • Run for minimum 2 weeks (capture weekly patterns)
  • Calculate required sample size using statistical power (typically 80%) and significance level (p < 0.05)
  • Segment results by user cohort, device, and viewing history depth
Pitfalls to Mention

Network effects (users share accounts), novelty effect (new things always get clicks initially), Simpson's Paradox (aggregate results can be misleading).

Estimation

Estimate the revenue Netflix loses each year from password sharing.

MEDIUM ▾
Structure

Revenue lost = (Shared users) × (Conversion rate to paid) × (ARPU)

Estimation Steps
  • Netflix has ~250M paid subscribers globally
  • Estimate ~30% of users share passwords → ~75M shared users
  • Of those, assume 30–40% would actually pay if forced → ~25M potential subscribers
  • Average ARPU globally: ~$11.5/month → $11.5 × 12 × 25M = ~$3.5B/year
Sanity Check

Netflix publicly stated they believe 100M+ households share passwords — this aligns. My estimate is conservative, suggesting real lost revenue could be $5–7B annually.

SQL / Analytics

Write a query to find users who signed up in the last 90 days but have not watched anything.

EASY ▾
SQL Answer

SELECT u.user_id, u.signup_date
FROM users u
LEFT JOIN watch_events w
  ON u.user_id = w.user_id
WHERE u.signup_date >= CURRENT_DATE - INTERVAL '90 days'
  AND w.user_id IS NULL;

Business Insight

These are "activated but inactive" users — highest-risk churn segment. Trigger an email: "You haven't tried Netflix yet — here's what's trending in your area."

Growth Strategy

Netflix's subscriber growth has plateaued in the US. How would you grow it?

HARD ▾
Growth Framework

Use the Ansoff Matrix: Market Penetration → Market Development → Product Development → Diversification.

Levers
  • Penetration: Win back churned users (17% return within 12 months) — targeted win-back offers
  • Market Dev: Expand live content (sports, events) — new audience segments
  • Product Dev: Gaming, interactive content, social watch parties
  • Diversification: Netflix theme parks, merchandise, theatrical releases
Prioritize by

Impact × Feasibility × Time-to-revenue. Quick win = win-back campaigns. Big bet = live sports rights (NFL, IPL).

Prioritization

You have 3 product features to build but only 1 engineer for 2 weeks. How do you decide what to build?

EASY ▾
Framework: RICE Score

RICE = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort
Rank each feature on this score and build the highest RICE first.

Also Consider
  • Strategic alignment — does it match the OKR for the quarter?
  • Dependencies — does Feature B unblock other teams?
  • Risk — what's the downside if it doesn't work?
Show Process

"I'd score all 3 features on RICE, share the table with the PM and stakeholders, and make the call transparent — not just based on gut feeling."

Interview Execution Tips

Knowing the framework is 50%. Delivering it confidently under pressure is the other 50%.

🔇

Think Out Loud

Interviewers want to see your thinking process, not just the final answer. Narrate every step: "I'm going to structure this as a tree with 4 branches..."

🎯

Always Clarify First

Never start answering immediately. Ask 2–3 clarifying questions. It signals maturity and prevents wasted effort on the wrong problem.

📐

MECE Everything

Every time you list reasons or solutions, make sure they don't overlap and together cover all cases. Practice this until it's instinctive.

💡

Hypothesis Before Data

State what you think is happening before asking for data. This shows you can think analytically without needing a dashboard to tell you what to think.

📊

Quantify When Possible

Don't say "churn increased." Say "churn increased from 2.1% to 2.8% monthly — that's roughly 500K lost subscribers at $13/month ARPU." Numbers show analytical rigor.

⚡

End With a Clear Recommendation

Never end with "it depends." End with: "Based on this analysis, my top recommendation is X because Y. I'd measure success by Z." Be decisive.

🔄

Acknowledge Trade-offs

For every recommendation, mention one downside. This shows intellectual honesty and business maturity — qualities analysts need.

🗣️

Use Business Language

Replace "I would look at numbers" with "I'd pull a cohort retention curve segmented by acquisition channel." Precise vocabulary signals expertise.
